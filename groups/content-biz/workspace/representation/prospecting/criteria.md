# Creator Prospecting Criteria

Criteria for finding new creators to represent.

## Pre-Filters

```
minFollowers: 10000
maxDaysInactive: 14
requireEnglish: true
```

## must_have

- **Growing audience**: Recent follower growth trend
- **Consistent posting**: At least weekly (or regular schedule)
- **Technical/educational content**: AI, engineering, dev tools, system design
- **Not currently represented**: Open to working with us
- **Open to monetization**: Interested in sponsorships

## nice_to_have

- Already doing some sponsored content (knows the process)
- Multiple platforms with audience (newsletter + social)
- Engaged community (comments, shares, not just views)
- Has email list or owned audience
- Humor or distinctive voice
- Production quality (good audio/video for YouTube)

## exclude_if

- Already represented exclusively by another agency
- Declining audience trends
- Controversial content history
- Low engagement rate for follower count
- Primarily promotional/affiliate (not educational)

## Target Creator Profiles

### Tier 1 (Primary Focus)

**AI/ML Educators**
- Teaching production AI, ML engineering
- Examples: Paul Iusztin, Yacine Mahdid
- Platforms: Newsletter, YouTube, LinkedIn

**System Design / Backend**
- Infrastructure, distributed systems, backend patterns
- Examples: Neo Kim
- Platforms: Newsletter, YouTube

**Tech Comedy (Rare)**
- Humor + technical insight
- Examples: Kai Lentit, Fireship
- Platforms: YouTube, X

### Tier 2 (Opportunistic)

**DevRel / Developer Advocates**
- Current or former DevRel looking to build personal brand
- Often have good content instincts

**Technical Writers / Journalists**
- Tech journalism background
- Newsletter-focused

## Outreach Approach

1. Identify creators matching criteria
2. Understand their current monetization (if any)
3. Pitch value: more sponsors, better matches, less work for them
4. Start with one sponsor partnership as proof of value
5. Expand relationship over time

## Red Flags

- Only responds to DMs for "collab" (usually looking for free promotion)
- Inconsistent content schedule
- Heavy reliance on trending topics vs. evergreen
- Poor engagement despite high follower count
- Already working with many sponsors (saturated)
