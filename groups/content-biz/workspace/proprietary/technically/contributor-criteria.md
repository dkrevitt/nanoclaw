# Contributor Criteria: Technically

## Current Search: "The Analyst"

Looking for a correspondent to be the voice of Technically content going forward. Technically explains what tech companies do + how they make money, and we want to do much more data-driven analysis content.

**Target:** 5 candidates for pilot posts → hire 1-2

## Pre-Filters

minFollowers: 0 (audience size doesn't matter - looking for talent + potential)
maxDaysInactive: 30
requireEnglish: true
requireTimezone: Americas

## must_have

- **Opinionated**: Willing to offer takes on technologies, tech companies
- **Funny**: Can't take themselves too seriously
- **Analytically minded**: Some type of data analysis background
- **Technically curious**: Interested in tech companies, tech products, startups
- **Available**: Must be freelancer (researcher/writer/analyst, software engineer) or student. Not a fit for someone with a full-time job (given 20hr/week commitment)
- **Comfortable on camera**: Posts about themselves, comfortable sharing face on the internet

## nice_to_have

- Publishes memes / humor content on X / LinkedIn / Instagram
- Publishes videos on YouTube / Instagram / X
- Has a presence on Hacker News / Reddit (accounts are anon, harder to track)
- Has written long-form content (blog posts, tutorials, essays)
- Active in relevant communities

## exclude_if

- Has a full-time job (time commitment won't work)
- Primarily promotional/affiliate content
- No original work or takes
- Outside Americas timezones

## Potential Background Fits

1. **Freelance writer / journalist** covering tech
2. **Academic background** - grad school student, researcher (discipline matters less - could be economics or any discipline)
3. **Finance background** - banker, consultant, accountant, analyst
4. **Technical / industry background** - engineer or DevRel who wants to do more writing
